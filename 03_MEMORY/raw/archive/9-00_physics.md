Assistant ManagerAh, I see. You are referring to the Orbital Phase Angle used for transfer windows (like in Kerbal Space Program).
Short answer: Mathematically, yes. The operation is identical (subtraction of angles). Functionally, no. They measure different things.
KSP Phase Angle (Intercepts): Measures the angle between Two Planets (e.g., Earth vs. Mars) relative to the Sun. It tells you when to launch.
Our 1D Check (Visibility): Measures the angle between Your Eye (Camera direction) and The Planet relative to your position. It tells you if you can see it.
ProgrammerThey share the same underlying math: Angular Difference.
**Orbital Phase Angle (KSP):**This is used to calculate Hohmann Transfers. \\phi = \\nu\_{target} - \\nu\_{origin}
Inputs: The Heliocentric Longitude of the Target (Mars) and the Origin (Earth).
Goal: Wait until this difference matches a specific "Transfer Window" value (e.g., ~44 degrees for Earth to Mars).
**Our 1D Visibility Check:**This is used for Frustum Culling. \\Delta \\theta = | \\theta\_{target} - \\theta\_{look_vector} |
Inputs: The Heliocentric Longitude of the Target (Mars) and the Heading of your Telescope.
Goal: Check if this difference is less than your field of view (FOV).
EngineerTo put it in the "Clock Face" analogy:
Orbital Phase Angle: You ask, "Is the minute hand (Earth) and the hour hand (Mars) separated by 15 minutes?" If yes, launch the rocket.
1D Visibility Check: You ask, "Is the hour hand (Mars) near the number 12 (Where I am looking)?" If yes, render the planet.
WriterLore-wise, this is the difference between the Navigation console and the Sensor console.
Navigation (Orbital Phase): "Captain, Mars is coming into alignment. Transfer window opens in T-minus 4 days."
Sensors (1D Check): "Captain, main sensors are sweeping Sector 4. Mars detected on the visual feed."
