# Page Title: Maritime Logistics Corridors

**Summary**: An overview of the Suez-Indo-Pacific corridor and the specific navigational and administrative protocols governing passage in May 2026.

**Sources**: [[frag-0249-suez.md]]

**Last updated**: 2026-05-24

---

The Suez Canal serves as the primary artery for transit between the Mediterranean and the Indian Ocean. Despite geopolitical volatility in the region, the canal maintains an "Operational" status governed by the Suez Canal Authority (SCA) Rules of Navigation (source: frag-0249-suez.md).

### Navigational Protocols

- **Convoy Organization**: Southbound transit operates in two daily convoys (source: frag-0249-suez.md).
- **Communication**: Vessels must radio the harbor when within 15 nautical miles of the Fairway Buoy at Port Said (source: frag-0249-suez.md).
- **Transit Speed**: Regulated at 8 knots to minimize bank erosion; transit typically requires 11 to 16 hours (source: frag-0249-suez.md).

### Operational Environment

The de facto closure of the Strait of Hormuz since February 2026 has significantly altered global shipping, forcing rerouting and causing vessel bunching at Mediterranean hubs (source: frag-0249-suez.md). While the canal remains open, the administrative and scheduling environment is increasingly congested as authorities prioritize essential commodities (source: frag-0249-suez.md).
