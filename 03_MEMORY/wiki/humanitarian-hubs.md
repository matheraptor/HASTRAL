# Page Title: Humanitarian Hubs

**Summary**: Consolidated logistics and cargo requirements for Port Sudan, Djibouti, and Colombo, acting as critical humanitarian lifelines.

**Sources**: [[frag-0249-sudan.md]], [[frag-0249-djibouti.md]], [[frag-0249-colombo.md]]

**Last updated**: 2026-05-24

---

Intermediate stop-overs are essential for refueling, crew changes, and aid distribution in the fragmented maritime environment of May 2026.

### Port Sudan (Sudan)

Port Sudan is the primary "lifeline" for a severe famine crisis affecting 33.7 million people (source: frag-0249-sudan.md).

- **Needs**: Therapeutic foods (RUTF, F-75/F-100), WASH infrastructure kits (chlorine), vaccine cold-chain equipment, and fuel for humanitarian convoys (source: frag-0249-sudan.md).
- **Constraints**: Persistent terminal congestion, with truck dispatch delays (~48 hours) (source: frag-0249-sudan.md).

### Djibouti (Horn of Africa)

Djibouti serves as the gateway to landlocked Ethiopia, handling >90% of its trade (source: frag-0249-djibouti.md).

- **Needs**: Bulk fertilizer, food staples (vegetable oil, Super Cereal Plus), and energy (jet fuel/diesel) (source: frag-0249-djibouti.md).
- **Protocols**: "Fast-Track" protocols exist for Ethiopian-bound cargo via the electrified railway (source: frag-0249-djibouti.md).

### Colombo (Sri Lanka)

A vital transshipment hub, but currently faces significant administrative bottlenecks (source: frag-0249-colombo.md).

- **Challenges**: Military inspections have significantly slowed clearance rates, leading to perishable food loss (source: frag-0249-colombo.md).
- **Opportunities**: Consolidation of stranded relief cargo for Southeast Asia/Japan and deep-water capability (source: frag-0249-colombo.md).
