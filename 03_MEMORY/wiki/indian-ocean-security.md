# Page Title: Indian Ocean Security

**Summary**: Meteorological shifts, India's naval assertion, and regional piracy threats.

**Sources**: [[frag-0249-indianocean.md]], [[frag-0249-piracy.md]]

**Last updated**: 2026-05-24

---

Crossing the Indian Ocean introduces complex climate drivers and power competition.

### Meteorological Drivers

- **Southwest Monsoon**: Onset in May 2026 brings heavy rainfall to the Bay of Bengal (source: frag-0249-indianocean.md).
- **Super El Niño**: The dominant climate driver, causing hazardous, sudden bursts of wind activity that can reverse trade winds and create hazardous sea states (source: frag-0249-indianocean.md).

### Geopolitical Security

India has asserted its role as a "net security provider" under the SAGAR vision (source: frag-0249-indianocean.md).

- **Safe Corridors**: Indian carrier battle groups provide close protection for critical shipping, with a policy of allowing Indian-flagged vessels to transit without "protection fees" to foreign entities (source: frag-0249-indianocean.md).
