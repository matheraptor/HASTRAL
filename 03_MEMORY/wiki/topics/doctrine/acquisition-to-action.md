# Acquisition-to-Action Doctrine

The **Acquisition-to-Action Doctrine** positions Hastral as a hybrid entity, capable of functioning as both a private military company and a humanitarian enterprise (source: 7-00_doctrine.md).

## Dual-Use Philosophy

This philosophy allows a single asset to serve multiple roles simultaneously. For example, an asset might provide Gaza relief, mitigate flooding in Valencia, and conduct anti-piracy missions in a single deployment. This versatility is key to attracting diverse investors, from defense-focused firms like Shield Capital to impact investors like the Omidyar Network.

### Strategic Asset Procurement

The doctrine emphasizes the acquisition of versatile, proven platforms, such as the **Freedom-class Littoral Combat Ship (LCS)**. These vessels are acquired from decommissioned US Navy fleets and refitted in Livorno, Italy, to include advanced systems (e.g., HELIOS directed-energy system).
