# The Artifexian Method

**Summary**: A 4-phase procedural and artistic methodology for generating realistic land topography, synthesizing geological science with vector art execution.

**Sources**: 8-08_skill_artifexianWorldbuilder.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

---

The Artifexian methodology for world map creation synthesizes the procedural rigor of scientific inquiry and the nuanced skill of artistic interpretation. By grounding creative choices in geological history and data-driven calculations, it constructs continents that are aesthetically pleasing and deeply believable.

## Workflow Phases

The workflow is divided into four distinct phases:

1. **Data Export & Project Setup**: Extracting the tectonic blueprint of the world from GPlates and organizing the raw data within a vector graphics editor. This phase heavily relies on specific Vector Topography constraints.
2. **Scientific Calculation**: Utilizing the Worldsmith Spreadsheet to translate geological data into concrete elevation values.
3. **Artistic Execution**: Using the calculated values and geological principles to draw the final, detailed topography via organic detailing techniques.
4. **Ocean Bathymetry**: Applying a similar methodology to carve out trenches, ridges, and abyssal plains (to be completed post-land topography).

## Key Considerations

- **Drawing Tools**: It is strongly recommended to use a stylus and a drawing tablet. This allows for fluid motions and prevents repetitive strain injury (RSI).
- **Deliberate Omissions**: Complex geological features, such as fjords, are intentionally omitted from the initial topography pass and postponed to a later stage to manage complexity.
