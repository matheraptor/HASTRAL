## The Elite Benchmark

Modern 3D graphics often rely on complex GPUs and abstraction layers, but the core principles remain unchanged from the early era of computing.

- **Efficiency**: 1980s games like _Elite_ (David Braben & Ian Bell) achieved fluid 3D motion on low-powered hardware (BBC Micro) using clever mathematical tricks rather than raw power.
- **Wireframe Heritage**: The move toward shaded polygons and textures followed years of optimization in representing 3D shapes on 2D screens.
- **HASTRAL Alignment**: The "Shelder Method" aligns with this heritage by prioritizing mathematical elegance and data-driven rendering over brute-force geometry.
