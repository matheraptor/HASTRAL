# Task Force Raptor: Maritime Logistics & Operations

**Summary**: Task Force Raptor (TF Raptor) is the primary maritime operational command integrating civilian PMC assets, allied naval units, and infrastructure deployment. Operating 2026 South China Sea theater with dual humanitarian/intelligence missions.

**Sources**: SITREP_202601291400.pdf, Okinawa_delivery.pdf
**Last updated**: 2026-02-06

## Command Structure & Assets

### Core Fleet Composition

**HTP L9706 "Hestia Cradle"** (Primary Vessel)

- Landing Platform Dock (LPD); well-deck capable of carrying LCU Landing Craft Utility
- Battery swap capacity: lithium-ion arrays for regional "Smart Grid" restoration
- Current loadout: 34 ISO fuel tanks (F76), aviation fuel (JP5), medical computing infrastructure
- Operational status: Berthed at White Beach, Okinawa (as of 02/06/2026)

(source: SITREP_202601291400.pdf)

**HTP A9805 "Diego Marea"** (Secondary Vessel)

- Capacity for 50 tons high-nutrient rice (humanitarian cargo) + infrastructure servers
- ETA Manila North Harbor: February 4, 0600 PHT
- ETA Okinawa Naha Port: February 7, 1200 Z
- Current mission: "Raptor-Vector" logistical support vessel

(source: Manila_delivery.pdf, Okinawa_delivery.pdf)

**ROKN DDG995 "Jeongjo the Great"** (Allied Naval Unit)

- South Korean Navy destroyer integrated into TF Raptor command
- Capabilities: SPY1 radar systems, Seahawk helicopter operations, tactical coordination
- Current mission: Escort and picket operations; supporting aviation fuel logistics
- Status: Departure from TF Raptor for return to ROK February 7, 2026

(source: SITREP_202601291400.pdf)

### Aviation Assets

**S70B Seahawk "Mossy"** (Primary Rotorcraft)

**FGZMK Dassault Falcon 8X** (Executive Transport)

- High-speed business jet for personnel extraction
- Equipped for private FBO operations at international airports
- Current use: Hastral executive transport Manila → Tokyo (4.5 hour cruise)

## Operational Constraints & Geopolitical Context

### South China Sea Challenges

- Chinese Maritime Militia presence: ~1,900 "fishing vessels" between Manila and Taiwan
- GPS jamming detected in Manila harbor (low-level tactical warning)
- PLA Southern Theater Command air/naval patrols active around Scarborough Shoal
- Philippine Coast Guard pressure to fast-track Code of Conduct with Beijing

(source: Manila_delivery.pdf)

### Diplomatic Cover Requirements

- TF Raptor operates under Board of Peace "Humanitarian Infrastructure" mandate
- Non-military civilian PMC alignment allows Philippine Coast Guard "middle ground" without appearing as US Navy deployment
- Compliance audits threaten to expose dual-use infrastructure nature of cargo

(source: Manila_delivery.pdf)
