# Mission FRAG-040

**Summary**: A strategic humanitarian transit and relief logistics operation involving the A9805 Diego Marea, transporting critical aid from Limassol to Aomori via the Suez-Indo-Pacific Corridor.

**Sources**: 1-.hastral-ops-0249.md

**Last updated**: 2026-06-10

---

## Overview
Mission FRAG-040 is a response to the "triple emergency" of 2026, characterized by a surge in humanitarian need, global economic shocks, and multi-theater conflict. The mission utilizes the A9805 Diego Marea to deliver relief cargo to Northern Japan following the Sanriku Earthquake, while maximizing impact through strategic stop-overs.

## Transit Route & Stop-overs
The vessel follows the Suez-Indo-Pacific Corridor, departing from Limassol, Cyprus.

### 1. Port of Colombo, Sri Lanka
- **Rationale**: Primary transshipment hub for South Asia.
- **Action**: Load stranded relief containers (UNICEF, IFRC, World Vision).
- **Cargo**: ~3,000 MT of perishable food and medical kits delayed since early 2026.
- **Destination**: Aomori/Japan or Southeast Asia hubs.

### 2. Port of Singapore / Port Klang, Malaysia
- **Rationale**: Critical node for the Myanmar humanitarian crisis.
- **Action**: Load Assistive Devices & WASH Materials.
- **Cargo**: ~500 MT of walking aids, physiotherapy equipment, and hygiene kits for survivors of the 2025 Myanmar earthquake and conflict-displaced populations.

## Operational Mandates
- **Security**: High-alert watch for Somali Pirate Action Groups (PAGs) in the Somali Basin; adherence to BMP protocols.
- **Meteorology**: Precautions for the Southwest Monsoon (Kerala onset May 26) and Super El Niño transition.
- **Diplomacy**: Broadcast status as "Cargo Food for Relief" to maintain "Safe Corridor" eligibility under the [[sagar-doctrine]] and IRGC vetting systems.
