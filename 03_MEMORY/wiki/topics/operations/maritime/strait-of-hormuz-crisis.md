# Strait of Hormuz Crisis (2026)

The closure of the Strait of Hormuz in 2026 represents one of the most significant disruptions to global maritime trade and energy security in the modern era.

## Systemic Impacts

- **Energy Security**: The closure disrupted approximately 20% of the world's global oil exports, leading to surging fuel and energy prices.
- **Humanitarian Supply Chains**: Airspace closures across the Gulf and Iraq, combined with the maritime blockade, severely delayed the delivery of critical nutrition, medical, and emergency supplies.
- **Economic Shock**: The "triple emergency" (combined with other regional crises) created a global economic shock, increasing operational costs for humanitarian programming.

## Diplomatic Responses

- **IMO Intervention**: Secretary-General Arsenio Dominguez has called for the urgent establishment of a "safe-passage framework" to protect civilian maritime workers and ensure the flow of aid.
- **Selective Blockade**: Iran has pursued a selective blockade, notably allowing Japanese ships to transit the strait to maintain critical energy imports for Japan.
- **Regional Power Projection**: India has asserted its maritime power to ensure the security of energy shipping lanes.
