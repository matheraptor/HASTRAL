# Global Maritime Crisis & Port Congestion (2026)

**Summary**: 2026 has witnessed severe port congestion, geopolitical disruptions of critical sea lanes (Red Sea, Strait of Hormuz), and cascading effects on global supply chains and humanitarian operations.

**Sources**: `2-20260518_news.md` (parts 1-5)

**Last updated**: 2026-06-10.

---

## Port Congestion Overview

The global shipping industry faces unprecedented delays in 2026 due to multiple converging factors:

- **Capacity constraints**: Major container ports overwhelmed by supply chain backlog.
- **Routing inefficiencies**: Traditional lanes disrupted; vessels forced into longer alternate routes.
- **Cost escalation**: Port fees and congestion surcharges rising; fuel costs increasing.

(source: FreightAmigo, 2026 Port Congestion)

**Impact on shippers**:

- Extended lead times (weeks to months beyond normal schedules)
- Increased operational costs
- Inventory accumulation at ports
- Risk of cargo spoilage (especially perishables)

## Triple Emergency Context
The maritime environment of May 2026 is defined by a "triple emergency": a surge in humanitarian need, a global economic shock, and a multi-theater conflict landscape affecting Sudan, the Horn of Africa, Myanmar, and Japan. (source: 1-.hastral-ops-0249.md)

## Critical Chokepoints

### Red Sea & Gulf of Aden

The Red Sea remains a critical chokepoint carrying ~12% of global trade and 30% of container traffic. However, security threats have disrupted normal operations.

**Houthi Attacks**: The Houthis (Iran-backed militia in Yemen) have conducted sustained attacks on commercial vessels transiting the Red Sea and Bab el-Mandeb Strait. (source: MARAD Advisory 2026-006)

**International Response**:

- US Department of Transportation (MARAD) issuing advisories to mariners
- International Maritime Organization (IMO) condemning attacks and calling for coordinated international protection
- Increased military escorts and defensive measures

### Strait of Hormuz

Japan reports heavy dependency on Hormuz transits: ~90% of crude oil imports flow through this 21-nautical-mile chokepoint. Iran's selective blockade/closure has forced major rerouting.

**Japan's Workaround**: Japan has been granted selective transit privileges for its flagged vessels, but this creates uncertainty and delays for other shippers.

(source: IRC Report on Humanitarian Supply Chain Collapse, 2026)
