# Project ADELE: Covert Infrastructure Deployment

**Summary**: Project ADELE is a multi-phase surveillance and infrastructure deployment operation utilizing humanitarian logistics as operational cover. The Argus-7 Surveillance Net represents the core intelligence architecture, deployed globally under Board of Peace "Medical Computing" mandates.

**Sources**: SITREP_202601291400.pdf, Manila_delivery.pdf, Okinawa_delivery.pdf
**Last updated**: 2026-02-06

---

## Operation Overview

Project ADELE operates through a sophisticated three-layer approach:

1. **Humanitarian Front**: Medical computing kits, pharmacy modules, and healthcare infrastructure as operational cover
2. **Surveillance Infrastructure**: Argus-7 "Sniffer" units deployed at critical communications convergence points
3. **Intelligence Collection**: Satellite-linked monitoring nodes positioned in strategically sensitive regions

(source: Manila_delivery.pdf)

The operation leverages the Board of Peace's "Humanitarian Infrastructure" mandate to provide diplomatic cover for infrastructure deployment that would otherwise violate sovereignty concerns.

## The Argus-7 Surveillance Net

### Operational Deployment

The Argus-7 units ("Sniffers") are deployed at critical fiber convergence points. Initial 2026 deployment focused on:

- **Makati Communications Gateway (MCG)**: San Antonio district intersection where Manila's major fiber lines converge
- **Board-certified status** provides immunity from maritime intercept and port inspection (source: Manila_delivery.pdf)

### Cover Narrative

Officially designated as "Medical Computing" infrastructure for:

- Remote healthcare delivery
- Disaster relief pharmacy modules
- Smart grid restoration in post-crisis regions

Operationally, these nodes create an unattributed intelligence collection network immune to attribution or legal challenge under BoP frameworks.

## Strategic Implications

### The Compliance Audit Problem

Board of Peace "Supply Chain Transparency" mandates require random audits to verify no subsidized Chinese components in "Medical" server clusters. Project ADELE relies on:

- Air-gapped encryption ("black box" security protocols)
- Deniable dual-use infrastructure framing
- Multi-layer obfuscation (humanitarian → medical → infrastructure)

Risk: Direct inspection would reveal Argus-7 true purpose as unattributed intelligence collection. (source: Manila_delivery.pdf)

### The Social Media Stratagem

Elvin developed a Bluesky strategy (@matheraptor account) to "leak" operation truth through gaming/writing community channels, framing the mission as logistical storytelling rather than intelligence deployment. This provides:

- Credibility in creative communities immune to corporate/state filters
- Narrative control prior to official disclosure
- Community "boost" to establish plausible deniability (source: Manila_delivery.pdf)
