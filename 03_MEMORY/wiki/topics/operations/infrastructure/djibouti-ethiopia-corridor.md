# Djibouti-Ethiopia Corridor

Djibouti serves as the critical maritime gateway for landlocked Ethiopia, handling over 90% of its import and export trade.

## Strategic Infrastructure

- **Port Capacity**: Djibouti's deep-sea ports maintain sufficient capacity to manage rising cargo volumes without significant congestion.
- **Logistics Hub**: The nation is actively evolving into a regional logistics hub, leveraging its strategic position.
- **Connectivity**: The Addis Ababa–Djibouti Corridor, featuring modern highways and an electrified railway, has significantly reduced transit times.

## Operational Priorities

- **Ethiopian Cargo Priority**: The Djibouti Ports and Free Zones Authority (DPFZA) prioritizes cargo destined for Ethiopia, with a particular focus on essential supplies like fertilizer.
