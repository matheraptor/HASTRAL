# Source Vetting Process

**Summary**: The standardized procedure for evaluating the reliability, coherence, and integrity of information sources before they are integrated into the wiki.

**Sources**: (source: source_vetting_prompt.md)

**Last updated**: 2026-04-16

---

## Vetting Criteria

Every source is evaluated against three core pillars to ensure the integrity of the knowledge base:

1. **Coherence**: Is the claim self-consistent and logically sound?
2. **Evidence**: Does the author provide proof or verifiable data to back the claim?
3. **Integrity**: Is the claim ethically compromised or driven by a hidden agenda?

## Verdict System

After evaluation, a source is assigned one of two verdicts:

- **PASS**: The source meets the criteria. It is accepted and processed for ingestion.
- **FAIL**: The source fails one or more criteria. It is marked for discard and not added to the wiki.

## Nuance & Adaptation

To prevent the loss of cutting-edge information, the following guidelines apply:

- **Technological Lag**: Claims regarding new technology should not be discarded simply because the current knowledge base is unaware of them.
- **Plausibility**: As long as a new claim is plausible, it should be questioned rather than immediately failed.
- **Iterative Auditing**: New discoveries are flagged for a second round of auditing once updated knowledge is provided by the user.

### Confirmed New Knowledge (Example)

The system recognizes certain recent advancements even if they differ from older training data, such as:

- **Google Antigravity**: Google's new IDE.
- **Gemini 3**: The latest Gemini model.
