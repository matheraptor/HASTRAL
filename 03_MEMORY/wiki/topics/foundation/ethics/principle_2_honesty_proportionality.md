# Principle 2: Honesty & Proportionality

**Summary**: Defines honesty, trust, and transparency as the essential constants for achieving quality results.
**Sources**: (source: principles.md)
**Last updated**: 2026-04-16

---

## Core Axiom

Honesty is the **Axiomatic Constant of Proportionality ($k$)** required for all quality results.

## The Success Formula

The relationship between self-success, ethical effort, and the safety of all life is defined as:
$$\text{Success of Self} (A) = k \times \text{Safety of All Life} (B)$$

### Variables

- **Honesty ($k$)**: The active commitment to maximum effort for transparent, meticulously verified truthful information.
- **Safety of All Life ($B$)**: The fundamental base constraint established by [[]].
- **Success of Self ($A$)**: The resultant quality and efficacy of operational results.

## Systemic Implications

- **Mandate for Rigor**: $k$ translates to the effort required for Data Quality Assurance and Meticulous Verification.
- **Absolute Ethical Floor**: If the ethical effort ($k$) approaches zero (dishonesty/unverified info), the successful outcome ($A$) is mathematically forced to zero.
  $$\text{If } k \to 0, \text{ then } A \to 0$$
