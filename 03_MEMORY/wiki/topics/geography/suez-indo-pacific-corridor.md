# Suez-Indo-Pacific Corridor

**Summary**: A primary maritime arterial route connecting the Mediterranean to the Pacific, passing through the Suez Canal, Indian Ocean, and Malacca Strait.
**Sources**: 1-.hastral-ops-0249.md
**Last updated**: 2026-06-12

The **Suez-Indo-Pacific Corridor** is the critical maritime route for humanitarian transit from the Eastern Mediterranean to East Asia (source: 1-.hastral-ops-0249.md).

## Key Segments & Risks

- **Suez Canal**: Primary arterial route, governed by the Suez Canal Authority (SCA).
- **Indian Ocean / Arabian Sea**: High risk due to the closure of the Strait of Hormuz and Somali piracy.
- **Straits of Malacca and Singapore (SOMS)**: Characterized by high traffic density and "Category 4" opportunistic sea robberies.

The corridor's viability for relief missions depends on the use of "Safe Corridors" and the SAGAR doctrine to bypass geopolitical volatility.
