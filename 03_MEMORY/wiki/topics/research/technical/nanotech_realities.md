# Nanotech Realities

**Summary**: A critical analysis of "nanobots" as microscopic, non-autonomous devices ("microrobots") requiring external guidance and power.

**Sources**: 11-00_Lore_00.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-17

---

Current "nanobot" technology is largely a misnomer for devices that lack internal propulsion or power systems. Most researched microrobots are in the micrometer to centimeter scale and function as "remote-controlled beads" moved by external magnetic fields or powered via fiber optic cables (source: 11-00_Lore_00.md).

## Technical Constraints

- **Propulsion**: Lack of internal motors; requires external magnetic steering.
- **Power**: Cannot operate in dark biological environments without external energy input (e.g., light via fiber optics).
- **Scale**: Often larger than true "nano" scale, reaching tens of micrometers.

## Practical Use Cases

- **Localized Drug Delivery**: Effective for surface-level infections (lungs, sinuses) where biofilms are present.
- **Biological Data Collection**: Gathering sensor data from specific anatomical sites.

## Gamedev Application: Hard Sci-Fi Mechanics

To maintain realism in hard sci-fi settings, nanotech should be depicted as requiring significant infrastructure:

- **Magnetic Anchors**: Players must stay within a magnetic field generator for "nanobot" healing.
- **External Tethers**: Repairs may require physical tethers or fiber-optic links rather than autonomous swarms.
- **Infrastructure Overload**: Avoiding the "grey goo" trope by emphasizing power and control requirements.
