## Operational Mandates & Control Mechanisms

### Humanitarian Infrastructure Program

The BoP operates a "Humanitarian Infrastructure" mandate that provides:

- Diplomatic cover for infrastructure deployment in sovereign nations
- Authority to inspect cargo under humanitarian pretense
- Ability to impose "Technical Holds" on vessels for compliance verification
- Unilateral declaration of zones as "Restricted Logistics Zones"

Example: Post-January 2026 Israeli airstrike surge led BoP to declare Beirut Port a "Restricted Logistics Zone," enabling detention of MV Hope of Tyche despite 100% cargo offload completion. (source: Okinawa_delivery.pdf)

### Compliance Audit Framework

**"Supply Chain Transparency" Mandate:**

- Random audits of Board-certified partner organizations
- Verification of no subsidized Chinese components in infrastructure
- Authority to seize devices/servers for "forensic audit" if undocumented systems detected
- Risk: Air-gapped encryption and dual-use infrastructure vulnerable to inspection

(source: Manila_delivery.pdf)

### Harbor Fee & Leverage System

The Board imposes "BoP Harbor Fees" for port access and vessel release:

- Serves as de facto revenue mechanism and compliance enforcement
- Required before clearance of vessels under Technical Hold status
- Example: MV Hope of Tyche release conditional on full fee payment after 24-hour mediation period (source: Okinawa_delivery.pdf)
