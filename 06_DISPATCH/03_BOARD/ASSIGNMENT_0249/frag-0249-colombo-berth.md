# ⚓ Logistics Solution: Mars-Class (AFS) Humanitarian Deployment (Port of Colombo)

## 🎯 Primary Berth Recommendation

* **Designated Location**: **Jaya Container Terminal (JCT) – Berth 2** (Backup: JCT Berth 1).
* **Physical Fit**: Vessel draft (8.5m) and length (177m) perfectly align with Berth 2’s depth (13.0m) and quay length (332m).
* **Operational Fit**: Run by the state-operated Sri Lanka Ports Authority (SLPA), offering adjacent conventional open-side sheds needed for non-containerized cargo.

## 🏗️ Cargo Discharge Strategy

* **Shipboard Handling**: Use the ship's own onboard boom cranes and internal forklifts to lift palletized breakbulk cargo from the 5 main holds to the deck.
* **Quayside Transfer**: Use port mobile harbor cranes or auxiliary hooks to lift pallets directly onto flatbed trailers, bypassing automated container gantry lanes.

## 🚁 Helicopter Airlift (VERTREP) Option

* **Bypass the Port**: Utilize the ship’s dual-helicopter flight deck from the Colombo outer harbor anchorage.
* **Direct Delivery**: Fly time-sensitive medical or emergency kits directly inland to staging areas designated by the Disaster Management Centre (DMC), avoiding all port congestion.

## 📋 Customs & Administrative Fast-Track

* **Consignee Labeling**: Address all manifests directly to a Sri Lankan Government Ministry or accredited UN/NGO agency.
* **Fee Waivers**: This specific consignee designation triggers automated customs clearance and secures mandatory demurrage fee waivers from the Ministry of Finance.
