# Potential Stop-over I: The Sudan Humanitarian Emergency

The first critical opportunity for an intermediate humanitarian stop-over is Port Sudan. As of May 2026, Sudan is the site of the world's most severe humanitarian crisis, with 33.7 million people—more than half the population—requiring assistance.[12] Conflict, mass displacement, and the collapse of basic services have pushed at least nine areas to the brink of famine.[12, 13] Port Sudan has emerged as the nation's primary "lifeline," serving as the hub for the World Food Programme (WFP), UNICEF, and the Logistics Cluster.[14]

## Operational Status of Port Sudan

Port Sudan is currently "Operational," serving as the arrival point for humanitarian airlifts and sea freight. The WFP-managed United Nations Humanitarian Air Services (UNHAS) maintains connections between Port Sudan, Nairobi, and Amman to facilitate the movement of aid workers.[14] The port terminal, however, experiences persistent congestion, with truck dispatch delays of approximately 48 hours due to high volumes of bulk grain and medical supplies being prioritized for the interior.[15]

### Specific Cargo Requirements for Sudan

A humanitarian vessel could significantly alleviate the crisis by delivering high-impact commodities. The "Severe Acute Malnutrition" (SAM) crisis among children is particularly acute, with over 825,000 children under five at risk of death.[13, 17] Specialized cargoes for Port Sudan should include:

* **Therapeutic Foods**: RUTF (Ready-to-Use Therapeutic Food), F-75, and F-100 therapeutic milk.[13]
* **Water and Sanitation (WASH)**: Chlorine tablets and repair kits for sanitation infrastructure, as 17 million people lack safe drinking water.[13]
* **Medical Supplies**: Vaccine cold-chain equipment and trauma kits to support the 40% of health facilities still functioning.[13]
* **Energy**: Fuel for humanitarian convoys, as the Logistics Cluster handles thousands of tons of supplies passing through hubs in Port Sudan, Gedaref, and Kosti.[14]
